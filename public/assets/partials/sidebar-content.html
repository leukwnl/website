My background spans software development at Malaysia’s national energy company, PETRONAS, and nonprofit tech initiatives, alongside independent projects in game development where I’ve worn hats as an organizer, developer, and designer.

Growing up in multi-ethnic, multicultural Malaysia gave me a front-row seat to how technology reshapes culture. As society modernized and became more connected, I saw both opportunities and challenges. Games, in particular, became a bridge to bond with friends from very different backgrounds. That perspective, combined with my background in theater, improv, and storytelling, continues to shape how I see technology: not just as infrastructure, but as a cultural medium that connects people and communities.

I’m especially interested in interactive and creative systems like multiplayer backends, scalable user/dev tools, and user experiences that feel alive. I enjoy building the infrastructure that supports collaboration and creation, and I aspire to work at the intersection of systems, design, and human experience, where engineering choices directly shape how people play, connect, and create.

Outside of work, you'll find me playing Dungeons and Dragons, riding my bicycle, or tending to my plants.
